What did you learn about design and user experience?

It is very important to create a website that is an experience for the user. This experience can be temporary and therefore not long term very valuable. There needs to be a clear purpose and outcome desired before a website is designed to optimize functionality.

Do you like design and user experience? Was it fun working on your site, the plan, and/or the wireframe? (If you hated it, that's OK too)

I enjoy wireframing and design. I really like think about what the intended audience will get out of their interaction with a book, a website, a movie, etc. I am really disappointed that I could not get my 3 columna to work. I think the user experience would have been much greater if I had all three intended columns.

What was your process? What worked and didn't work?

First I tried to decide what the website's main point should be as a gateway to all of my other work. I viewed index.html the way I have seen it used over and over again, as the home page. I tried to visual an overall look to the site - there is still so much I want to learn and show everyone. I tried wireframing, but I found it limiting. So I went in the backyard and sat on a deck chair. I tried to just let my mind go. I then visualized the page and did the wireframing second and then coded the site.

I really wanted the third column to line up and work. It contains the connection to all of my work and I think that people would see the purpose of the site very quickly if they could see these links immediately before scrolling down. I like to add a Boot to the header after my title.
