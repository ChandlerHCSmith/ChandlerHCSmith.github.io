Intermediate HTML & CSS

What do you think about web standards? Are they good or bad?

It seems like a good idea that we are all work on the same page. I know that in some ways this can limit creativity, however, from what I've seen on the web, there will continue to be break throughs and modifications.

Does the standard structure make sense to you? Why or why not?

They do seem to make sense to me. I like a set of items to follow and then we add personal touches. So, I guess I am saying that I still want some freedom.

Do you like the new HTML5 elements, such as <header>, <footer>, and <nav>? Why or why not?

They seem very clean to me and this seems nice at this point. Maybe I would a different view if I already had been programming for awhile.

Why is reset.css necessary?

Reset, resets the browsers default to make way for your specifications.

What browsers have you used? Did you ever notice that sites looked different in different browsers? Do you think it's good or bad that they do?

Safari, Firefox, and Chrome. Yes, I have noticed that the placement of elements are different and sometimes the look of an element will be changed. On occasions, there are pieces even missing, although this is rare.

In these cases I was the "consumer" and would have liked uniformity to help me spend less time looking for items.

Did you find any resources on your own that helped you better understand a topic? If so, please list it.

I really like: http://www.w3schools.com/html/default.asp
